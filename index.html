The Man the
System Forgot.

His name is Dyson. His mother named him after the tire — because she knew he was built to roll, to endure, to keep going even when everything around him was flat. And for the last two years, he proved her right in the hardest way possible.

He wasn't on the streets because he gave up. He survived in a stranger's garage because the system gave up on him. His family turned their backs. He had no car, no address, no financial foundation — just the phone in his hands and the knowledge in his head.

That phone? Dyson had modified it himself. He's a tech person — someone who knows how to make devices do things they were never supposed to do. That's not a hobby. That's a skill. That's a career waiting to happen. He just needs the ground beneath his feet to stand on.

Someone reached out a hand. Offered him a night inside. Then a weekend. Then a Chromebook. Tried to give him money — he refused it. Instead he took what little he had and did something remarkable: he got a haircut. He showed up for himself. He said: I'm not done.

The Story Behind This Page

How I Built This —
And How You Can Too.

I met Dyson sitting on the side of the street. I let him stay a night. Then a weekend. I bought him a Chromebook. He gave back the money I offered and spent it on a haircut instead. That told me everything I needed to know about who this man is.

I wanted to help him more — to give him a voice and a way to collect donations while he sits outside McDonald's on his Chromebook. So I went to Claude AI and described his story. I told it about the tire metaphor. About why humans need each other the way cars need tires. And it built this entire page.

You can do the exact same thing — for free — for anyone you know who is homeless, struggling, or needs a voice.

1
Go to Claude.ai

It's free to use. Create an account at claude.ai — no credit card needed to get started.

2
Tell the story in your own words

Don't worry about spelling or grammar. Just tell Claude who the person is, what happened to them, what they need, and why people should care. The more real and personal, the better.

3
Ask for a landing page

Tell Claude to build a compelling donation landing page. Ask for it to be powerful, emotional, and designed to move people. Ask for a GoFundMe button. That's it.

4
Create a GoFundMe campaign

Go to gofundme.com, write their story, set a goal, and get your campaign link. Paste it into the page Claude built.

5
Share everywhere

Post to Facebook, text it to friends, share it in community groups. You don't need to go viral — you just need the right people to see it.
